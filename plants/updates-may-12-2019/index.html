Updates - May 12, 2019

May 12, 2019 3 min read

Fermentation - Sauerkraut

Cat and I made a few batches of Sauerkraut over the last few weeks. It was super easy and delicious. We used a combination of the recipes found in Real Food Fermentation: Preserving Whole Fresh Food with Live Cultures in Your Home Kitchen by Alex Lewan and The Noma Guide to Fermentation, with help on some of the physical, technical tasks from Brad Leone’s videos.

One issue we ran into with the batch above was that we over-seasoned. We added too much turmeric, and as the sauerkraut reduced in volume, the turmeric became overpowering.

Fermentation - Pickles

We also made multiple batches of pickles. In the first batch, we included a jalapeno which added a great spicy flavor. In the second batch, we included a habanero which made them way too spicy. In the third batch, we kept it simple and made straightforward dill pickles.

Fermentation - Blueberries and Beets

Cat is trying to ferment blueberries to make lactoblueberries using the Recipe from Noma and to ferment beets to make beet kvass.

Plants - Basils

Our basil plants have been doing amazing! We’ve transplanted them other pots and they are thriving. I’m thinking we will be able to do enough pruning in a few weeks to start noticeably supplementing our herb collection.

Plants - Spinach, Thyme, Sage Starters

We planted Spinach, Thyme, and Sage starts which are not doing so well. I think that their soil got too dry from the light, our room wasn’t humid enough, and some plants drowned from overly-aggressive watering (even though I was very careful).

Next time I’m going to try a few tricks from Tench Acre Farm https://www.tenthacrefarm.com/starting-seeds-indoors/.

Plants - Sprouts

We made sprouts in Jars using Broccoli seeds for sprouts bought off amazon; these turned out amazing! They were ready to eat in 4 to 6 days, tasted amazing, and I was just taking them straight out of the jar for multiple meals.

I’m definitely going to continue to grow sprouts in jars, but I want to experiment with how we eat them. I was adding them to the top of fried rice-esque dishes, which was great, but I think they can be more of a focal point of different dishes. They are different work with than salad greens, which is more what I’m used to.

Meal Prep - Making lots of Burritos

We made 16 burritos a few weeks ago and froze most of them for lunches, which we took out of the freezer and warmed up for the subsequent weeks. Our strategy was simple and effective: we made a flavorful bean sauce, roasted veggies, and shredded cheese and put all of the ingredients in tortillas. A great element of this strategy was that Cat could focus on the bean sauce while I focused on roasting the veggies.

Moving!

We just signed a lease for a new apartment that we will move to at the end of August. The apartment is super nice, but it is smaller and doesn’t have an outdoor area for growing plants. Cat and I are planning on trying to optimize our indoor growing for this new situation with its new constraints. New ideas and experiments will be announced soon!
